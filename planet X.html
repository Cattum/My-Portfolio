Planet X

Planet X is a 2D strategy game. Players will take on the role of a leader of a virtual world nation, operating the country through the implementation of policies to achieve different outcomes. Players will balance national development with environmental protection.

Download Project Files

Project Introduction

Overview: The game narrative is multi-linear; at major event nodes, various values will be calculated to determine the direction of the nation's storyline.The game's progress is driven by events, and different incidents will occur along different timelines. These incidents will be related to the environment, society, and the resolution of human moral dilemmas.

Background: Players will rule over a nation that is a type of modern centralized democracy. The technology level is on par with the present day, but the nation faces a series of urgent issues such as environmental hazards, the the solidification of class structures and corruption, and international relationships. On Planet X, nations are divided into higher and lower states; Players will be randomly or manually categorized into these countries, with the player's nation categorized as a higher state. Every five years, this planet will host a United Nations assembly (Conference), where each country can present its dilemmas or offer assistance, thereby working towards building a better planet.
